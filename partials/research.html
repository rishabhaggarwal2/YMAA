Alzheimer's Research Scholarship Program

Founded by YMAA's UCLA chapter, ARSP will channel some of the brightest young minds at universities across the country to actively work on conducting either clinical or laboratory research related to the disease for a full academic year. We hope that this year of research will inspire a future generation of scholars and scientists to be at the forefront of developing successful treatments--or even a cure for this disease. Through donations via Go­FundMe and direct sponsorship (individual and organizational) we plan to sponsor exceptional undergraduate student researchers with a stipend of $1000 per academic year.

By the end of the year each recipient will complete the following work:

  • Create at least one piece of scientific literature to be published in YMAA's newsletter.
  • Produce a creative piece, either highlighting an element of their research or Alzheimer's Disease at large, that can be comprehended by the nonscientific community and youth.
  • Present their work with at least one poster/abstract session organized through their associated university
  • Draft a research proposal for further investigation based on the work that they have done in their ARSP placement.

The first 10 scholarships will be rewarded to students within UCLA labs through a pilot program, then based on the amount fundraised, we will create this program at other chapters who are associated with other Alzheimer's Disease Research Centers (ADRC's), at universities such as Harvard, Northwestern, UC Berkeley, and UC Irvine.
