Research Scholarship Program

The Alzheimer’s Research Scholarship Program (ARSP) was founded by YMAA's UCLA chapter in 2016 to reward students who have actively been conducting either humanities or life science research related to Alzheimer’s Disease with a $1000 stipend. Through this program we hope to inspire a future generation of scholars and scientists to be at the forefront of developing successful treatments-- or even a cure for this disease.


APPLICATIONS DUE MAY 20TH

Who can apply?

  • Must be registered as a student at a university/college during the regular academic year.
    • Only UCLA, UC Berkeley, Northwestern University, and Tulane University students qualify for 2017-18
  • Must secure a year-long research position with a Professor/PI for the 2017-18 academic year

By the end of the year...

By the end of the year each recipient will complete the following work:
  • Create an abstract to be published on YMAA's website that includes proposal for future studies
  • Write an article that is comprehendible to the nonscientific community and youth.
  • Present their work with at least one poster/abstract session organized through their associated university
